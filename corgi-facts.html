🐕 Corgi Facts

  • The name "Corgi" comes from Welsh, often translated as "dwarf dog."
  • There are two breeds: the Pembroke Welsh Corgi and the slightly larger Cardigan Welsh Corgi.
  • Corgis were originally bred as herding dogs and would nip at cattle's heels to move them.
  • Queen Elizabeth II owned more than 30 corgis during her reign.
  • Pembroke Corgis are often born with naturally short or no tails.
  • Despite their short legs, corgis are fast, athletic, and surprisingly good at agility sports.
  • Their fluffy rear has earned the affectionate nickname "corgi butt."
  • Corgis are double-coated and shed a lot — sometimes called "blowing their coat."

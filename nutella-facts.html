🍫 Nutella Facts

  • Nutella was created by Italian pastry maker Pietro Ferrero in the 1940s.
  • It was inspired by "gianduja," a chocolate-hazelnut paste made when cocoa was scarce after WWII.
  • The original version, called "Giandujot," was sold as a solid loaf to be sliced onto bread.
  • The name "Nutella" was first used in 1964 — "nut" plus the soft Italian ending "-ella."
  • Roughly 25% of the world's hazelnut supply goes into making Nutella.
  • World Nutella Day is celebrated every year on February 5th.
  • Each jar contains about 50 hazelnuts on average.
  • Nutella is sold in more than 160 countries around the world.
